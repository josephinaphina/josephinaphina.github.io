Our Bodies Are Not Yours


OUR BODIES ARE NOT YOURS
We do not care whether it’s a compliment or criticism
Our bodies are not yours to comment on
They are not for your gaze to sexualize and objectify
They are not for you to stare at or criticize
They are OURS
They are not a collection of separate parts
Of unfeeling objects for you to collect, possess, or control
You can’t order one without the other like a la carte items off a brunch menu
Our bodies are not for your sexual gratification
WHY are we still fighting for them?
Something so obvious but rarely considered
They are not yours to wonder what they’re like or what size we wear
Family, Friends, Colleagues, Strangers
They are not yours to analyze, think about, and especially not touch
You cannot objectify our body parts then tell us to cover them when it suits you
They are ours to care and make decisions for
WHY?
Because we were born into these vessels, and no one knows them like we do
Because we shouldn’t be subjected to your unwarranted opinions
Because we don’t want men touching our bodies without permission
WE ARE TRYING TO LOVE OUR BODIES
But it is exhausting navigating them through this world
WE ARE TRYING TO CONNECT WITH OUR BODIES
But it’s taking us a long time to get there
Because of all the comments, criticism, and projections that women are constantly subject to Because of advertisements and television and movies that only represent one body type
That tell us we’re too big, too small, too tall, too short, too dark, too pale
We’re never good enough, no matter what we look like
LITTLE GIRLS DESERVE BETTER
Than being made to believe what is most important is how others perceive them
How others feel about the proportions of their anatomy
Instead of how comfortable they feel in their own skin
TEENAGE GIRLS DESERVE BETTER
They deserve better than staring in the mirror in the morning and before bed
Wondering if they’re good enough
Questioning their clothing choices because they’re afraid of being labeled as something they don’t identify with
Because of someone else’s diluted and brainwashed opinion
They deserve to know that loving themselves is the only thing that matters
That loving your body and who you are is the best thing you can do
That until you love yourself everything else will be harder

Reminders for loving yourself regardless of society and opinions other than your own:
1. How you feel is more powerful than how you look
2. That one dimensional version of beauty, health, and happiness they try to sell us is a LIE 3. Every body is beautiful in its own way, own yours because it is yours and it is the only one you will ever have
4. Don’t dress for the male gaze, dress for yourself and other women who will actually notice your effort
5. Ignore societies expectations of what you can and can’t do
6. Do what makes you feel like the most authentic version of yourself
7. Love yourself and the others around you radically
8. They will tell you that you can’t, do it anyway
